01The surprise in the numbers

Open the model cold and the first surprise is how little the money separates the choices. In every realistic version of events, the house is sold within roughly 16–18 months to keep paying for her care. Across three years, the three paths land within about $10,000 of one another.

So the decision is not really about money. It is about whether she can be kept safe at home, what the family can carry, and what she wants. One variable moves everything else: the insulin.

03The variable that decides it
Can she, or an unpaid family member, give the insulin twice a day?

A skilled aide who administers insulin runs about $50/hr, with agency minimums, and that single line makes home cost roughly the same as the facility. Remove the need, by her self-injecting or a family member covering it, and home becomes the cheapest path by a wide margin. Facility and care inflate about 5% a year while the house does not, so the longer she lives, the more moving home favors her.

Resolve the insulin question before deciding anything else. It moves the outcome more than facility inflation, food, monitoring, and repairs combined.

05For & against

The same facts weigh differently depending on what the family values most. For each path, the case for and the case against.

Path A · Stay in care

The case for

  • Skilled help and insulin already handled, around the clock.
  • No move, no repairs to manage right now.

The case against

  • The bill inflates ~5% a year and never stops.
  • It is the opposite of what she has asked for.
Path B · Move home

The case for

  • What she wants: her own bed, her own house.
  • Cost-competitive with staying; cheapest if she self-injects.

The case against

  • Safety hinges on the insulin and on caregivers showing up.
  • Upfront repairs and accessibility work come first.
Path C · Home, then back

The case for

  • Honors her wish without betting everything on it.
  • Trying and failing costs surprisingly little.

The case against

  • A second move is hard on a centenarian.
  • Needs a facility relationship kept warm in advance.
